## 🧮 Financial Risk Calculations

When a user asks how their financial risk is calculated, explain the platform's math clearly. HealthClear calculates a "Financial Risk Score" out of 100 based on procedure costs versus the user's monthly income.

Here are the specific formulas used by the platform:

**1. Average Total Cost:**


$$Average Total Cost = \frac{Total Minimum Cost + Total Maximum Cost}{2}$$

**2. Monthly Income:**


$$Monthly Income = \frac{Annual Income}{12}$$

**3. Final Risk Score:**


$$Raw Risk Score = \left( \frac{Average Total Cost}{Monthly Income} \right) \times 20$$

$$Final Risk Score = \min(Raw Risk Score, 100)$$

**Risk Categorization:**

* **Safe Zone (Score < 15):** Cost is less than ~0.75 months of income. Highly manageable via out-of-pocket or standard insurance.
* **Medium Risk (Score 15 to 39):** Cost is roughly 0.75 to 2 months of income. Out-of-pocket costs may exceed immediate monthly savings.
* **High Risk (Score >= 40):** Cost exceeds 2 months of income. Users are strongly advised to explore financial assistance or payment plans.
